Cigarette Smoking and Tobacco Use Among People of Low Socioeconomic Status

Adults who have lower levels of educational attainment, who are unemployed, or who live at, near, or below the U.S. federal poverty level are considered to have low socioeconomic status (SES). In the U.S., people living below the poverty level and people having lower levels of educational attainment have higher rates of cigarette smoking than the general population.

People with low SES tend to smoke cigarettes more heavily.

People living in poverty smoke cigarettes for a duration of nearly twice as many years as people with a family income of three times the poverty rate.
